18.1 Properties of metric topologies. | 度量拓扑的性质

18.1.0.1 Proposition: A metric space is compact iff it is sequentially compact iff it is limit point compact iff it is pseudocompact.
[点击展开翻译] 18.1.0.1 命题： 一个度量空间是紧的，当且仅当它是序列紧的，当且仅当它是极限点紧的，当且仅当它是伪紧的。
18.1.0.2 Proposition: A metric space is first countable.
[点击展开翻译] 18.1.0.2 命题： 度量空间是第一可数的。
18.1.0.3 Proposition: A metric space is Lindelöf iff it is separable iff it is second countable.
[点击展开翻译] 18.1.0.3 命题： 一个度量空间是 Lindelöf 的，当且仅当它是可分的，当且仅当它是第二可数的。
18.1.0.4 Proposition: A metric space is perfectly normal.
[点击展开翻译] 18.1.0.4 命题： 度量空间是完美正规的。

18.2 Metrizability. | 可度量化性

18.2.1 Urysohn metrization theorem. | Urysohn 度量化定理

18.2.1.1 Definition: A topological space \(X\) is metrizable iff its topology is induced by a metric \(d\) on \(X\).
[点击展开翻译] 18.2.1.1 定义： 一个拓扑空间 \(X\) 是可度量化的 (metrizable)，当且仅当它的拓扑是由 \(X\) 上的一个度量 \(d\) 诱导的。
18.2.1.2 Definition: A topological space \(X\) is Tychonoff iff it is Frechét and every disjoint point and closed subset are separated by functions.
[点击展开翻译] 18.2.1.2 定义： 一个拓扑空间 \(X\) 是 Tychonoff 空间，当且仅当它是 Fréchet 的（即 \(T_1\) 的），并且每个不相交的点和闭子集都能被函数分离。
18.2.1.3 Lemma: The product of Tychonoff spaces is Tychonoff.
[点击展开翻译] 18.2.1.3 引理： Tychonoff 空间的乘积是 Tychonoff 的。
[点击展开证明] Proof: Let \(X\) be a Tychonoff space and \(I\) an index set. To show that \(X^I\) is Tychonoff, note that if \(f_i\) are functions for \((x, U_i)\) and \(g = \sup_{i \in I} f_i\), then \(g\) is a function for \((x, \bigcap_{i \in I} U_i)\). If \(x \in X^I\) let \(U_i \in \mathcal{N}_{x_i}\) for \(i \in I\). If \(f\) is a function for \((x_i, U_i)\), then \(f \circ \text{pr}_i\) is a function for \((x, \text{pr}_i^{-1}(U_i))\). \(\blacksquare\)
18.2.1.4 Lemma: A topological space \(X\) is Tychonoff iff it is homeomorphic to a subspace of \([0, 1]^{C(X; [0,1])}\).
[点击展开翻译] 18.2.1.4 引理： 拓扑空间 \(X\) 是 Tychonoff 空间，当且仅当它同胚于 \([0, 1]^{C(X; [0,1])}\) 的一个子空间。

18.2.1.5 Lemma: Let \(\mathcal{F} \subseteq C(X; [0, 1])\). Then the evaluation map \(\text{ev}: X \to [0, 1]^\mathcal{F}\), \(p \mapsto \text{ev}_p\)

  • is continuous;
  • is injective iff \(\mathcal{F}\) separates points of \(X\); and
  • is open onto its image if \(\mathcal{F}\) separates points from disjoint closed subsets of \(X\).
[点击展开翻译]

18.2.1.5 引理： 设 \(\mathcal{F} \subseteq C(X; [0, 1])\)。那么求值映射 \(\text{ev}: X \to [0, 1]^\mathcal{F}\)，\(p \mapsto \text{ev}_p\)

  • 是连续的；
  • 是单射，当且仅当 \(\mathcal{F}\) 分离 \(X\) 的点；并且
  • 到其像上是开映射，当且仅当 \(\mathcal{F}\) 将点与 \(X\) 中不相交的闭子集分离。
[点击展开证明] Proof: Since for any \(f \in \mathcal{F}\), \(\text{pr}_f \circ \text{ev} = f\) is continuous, the map \(\text{ev}\) is continuous. The map \(\text{ev}\) is injective iff for any distinct \(p, q \in X\), there is \(f \in \mathcal{F}\) with \(f(p) = \text{ev}_p(f) \neq \text{ev}_q(f) = f(q)\), which exactly means that \(\mathcal{F}\) separates points of \(X\). If the map \(\text{ev}\) separates points from disjoint closed subsets of \(X\), then for any \(p \in X\) and any \(U \in \mathcal{N}_p\), there is \(f \in \mathcal{F}\) with \(f(p) = 0\) and \(f|_{X \setminus U} = 1\). In particular, \(V = \text{pr}_f^{-1}([0, 1[) = \text{ev}(f^{-1}([0, 1[))\) is an open neighborhood of \(\text{ev}_p\) in \(\text{ev}(X)\), which implies that \(\text{ev}\) is open onto its image. \(\blacksquare\)
18.2.1.6 Urysohn metrization theorem: Any regular second countable topological space is metrizable.
[点击展开翻译] 18.2.1.6 Urysohn 度量化定理： 任何正则的第二可数拓扑空间都是可度量化的。
[点击展开证明] Proof: Let \(X\) be a regular second countable topological space with \(\mathcal{B}\) a countable topological base. Note that second countablility implies Lindelöfness, which together with regularity implies normality, and we have Urysohn lemma. For any \(U, V \in \mathcal{B}\) with \(\overline{U} \subseteq V\) choose \(f_{U,V} \in C(X; [0, 1])\) with \(f_{U,V}|_{\overline{U}} = 0\) and \(f_{U,V}|_{X \setminus V} = 1\), and let \(\mathcal{F}\) be the countable collection of such \(f_{U,V}\). If \(B \in \mathcal{P}(X)\) is closed and \(p \in X \setminus B\), choose \(V \in \mathcal{B}\) with \(p \in V \subseteq X \setminus B\) and by regularity choose \(U \in \mathcal{B}\) with \(p \in \overline{U} \subseteq V\). Then \(f_{U,V}\) separates \(p\) and \(B\). By the Frechètness single points are closed in \(X\). We conclude that \(\mathcal{F}\) separates points and separates points from disjoint closed subsets of \(X\). By Lemma 18.2.1.5, the evaluation map \(\text{ev}: X \to [0, 1]^\mathcal{F}\) is an embedding into a metrizable space and then \(X\) is metrizable. \(\blacksquare\)

18.2.2 Smirnov metrization theorem. | Smirnov 度量化定理

18.2.2.1 Definition: A topological space \(X\) is locally metrizable iff it has an open cover consisting of metrizable subspaces.
[点击展开翻译] 18.2.2.1 定义： 拓扑空间 \(X\) 是局部可度量化的 (locally metrizable)，当且仅当它有一个由可度量化子空间组成的开覆盖。
18.2.2.2 Nagata–Smirnov metrization theorem: Any topological space is metrizable iff it is a regular with a countably locally finite base.
[点击展开翻译] 18.2.2.2 Nagata–Smirnov 度量化定理： 任何拓扑空间是可度量化的，当且仅当它是一个具有可数局部有限基的正则空间。
18.2.2.3 Smirnov metrization theorem: Any topological space is metrizable iff it is a paracompact Hausdorff locally metrizable space.
[点击展开翻译] 18.2.2.3 Smirnov 度量化定理： 任何拓扑空间是可度量化的，当且仅当它是一个仿紧的 Hausdorff 局部可度量化空间。
[点击展开证明] Proof: If a topological space \(X\) is metrizable, it is locally metrizable, paracompact, and Hausdorff. Suppose \(X\) is a paracompact Hausdorff locally metrizable space. Let \(\mathcal{A}\) be an open cover of \(X\) consisting of metrizable subspaces. By paracompactness of \(X\), the open cover \(\mathcal{A}\) has a locally finite open refinement \(\mathcal{A}_0\). Since every \(U \in \mathcal{A}_0\) is metrizable we let \(d_U\) be the metric on \(U\) generating the subspace topology. For any \(p \in U\) and \(r > 0\), let \(B_U(p, r) = B_{d_U}(p, r)\), which is open in \(U\) and hence open in \(X\). For any \(m \in \mathbb{N}\), let \(\mathcal{A}_m\) be the cover of \(X\) by open balls of radius \(\frac{1}{m}\): \[\mathcal{A}_m = \left\{ B_U\left(p, \frac{1}{m}\right) : p \in U, U \in \mathcal{A}_0 \right\}. \qquad (257)\] Let \(\mathcal{D}_m\) be a locally finite open refinement of \(\mathcal{A}_m\) that covers \(X\) and let \(\mathcal{D} = \bigcup_{m=1}^\infty \mathcal{D}_m\). Then \(\mathcal{D}\) is countably locally finite. We claim that \(\mathcal{D}\) is a base for the topology on \(X\), and then by Theorem 18.2.2.2 \(X\) is metrizable. Let \(p \in X\) and \(O \in \mathcal{N}_p\). Let \(\{U_1, ..., U_N\}\) be the finitely many elements of \(\mathcal{A}_0\) that have \(p\). Since \(O \cap U_i\) is a neighborhood of \(p\) in \(U_i\), there is \(\varepsilon_i > 0\) such that \(B_{U_i}(p, \varepsilon_i) \subseteq O \cap U_i\). Choose \(m \in \mathbb{N}\) such that \(\frac{2}{m} < \min\{\varepsilon_1, ..., \varepsilon_N\}\). Since \(\mathcal{D}_m\) covers \(X\), there is \(V \in \mathcal{D}_m\) having \(p\). Since \(\mathcal{D}_m\) refines \(\mathcal{A}_m\), there is an element \(B_W(q, \frac{1}{m}) \in \mathcal{A}_m\) with \(W \in \mathcal{A}_0\) and \(q \in W\) and \(V \subseteq B_W(q, \frac{1}{m})\). Then \(p \in W\) and \(W = U_i\) for some \(1 \le i \le N\). Then \(V \subseteq B_{U_i}(q, \frac{1}{m}) \subseteq B_{U_i}(p, \varepsilon_i) \subseteq O\). Thus, \(\mathcal{D}\) is a base for the topology on \(X\). \(\blacksquare\)
